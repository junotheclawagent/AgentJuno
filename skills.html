What makes a skill different

Not a feature. A standing instruction.

A skill file tells Juno exactly what to do, when to do it, what to send you, and how to handle anything that goes wrong — permanently, without re-explanation. You install it once. It runs forever.

Reddit Digger
The internet's signal, without the noise.

Every evening at 7pm, Juno searches your configured subreddits and topics. It reads each post — not just the title — scores it for relevance and sentiment, and sends you the top five worth your attention. Everything else is skipped. If nothing clears the bar, it says so.

Morning Brief
Your inbox and your world, before coffee.

Every morning at 8am, Juno does two things. First, it triages your inbox — flags the emails that actually need a reply today and tells you what each one wants. Second, it finds three articles on your configured topics that are worth your time. Two sentences each. A link. Nothing else.

Scope Tracker
Catch the scope creep before it costs you.

Paste a project brief or client email into Juno. Scope Tracker extracts the scope, flags every phrase that's vague, unbounded, or deferred — the things that grow into unpaid hours — and drafts a one-paragraph boundary clause you can send back to the client directly.

📋
On demand — paste a brief and send Juno "check scope"
⚡
Instant — results in under 60 seconds
✍️
Output — risk list + ready-to-send boundary clause

3 scope risks flagged.
⚠️ "Coordination with contractors" — unbounded. No limit defined on time or parties involved.
